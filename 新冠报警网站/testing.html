COVID-19 Testing:

Get tested if you have COVID-19 symptoms, include fever, cough, sore throat, shortness of breath, runny nose, loss of taste or smell, fatigue, acute blocked nose (congestion), muscle pain, joint pain, headache, diarrhoea, nausea/vomiting, loss of appetite, unexplained chest pain and conjunctivitis. It is important to maintain high testing rates to identify as many cases in the community as quickly as possible.



Now the government provides three different test methods:

COVID-19 testing at home

making an appointment

Go to the hospital by yourself

If you need a COVID-19 test and can't go to a testing clinic, contact your GP to discuss if they can arrange home testing.


If your GP is unable to arrange COVID-19 home testing, your local health district may be able to assist you. This service may be limited to people who cannot leave their home due to disability, injury, chronic health issues, or frailty affecting mobility.


For further information contact your local health district:
